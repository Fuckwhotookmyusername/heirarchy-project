🔍 Corporate Power Facts

  • 🔹 1. Most major corporations are top-down hierarchies.
    CEOs and boards of directors make decisions while lower-level employees have little say in operations or strategy.
  • 🔹 2. Stockholders often have more say than workers.
    Public companies prioritize shareholder profit, often at the expense of employee well-being or fair wages.
  • 🔹 3. Corporate lobbying shapes laws in their favor.
    Many corporations spend millions lobbying Congress to gain tax breaks, avoid regulation, or eliminate competition.
  • 🔹 4. Monopoly and market consolidation reduce competition.
    Tech giants like Amazon, Meta, and Google acquire competitors, reducing market diversity and centralizing power.
  • 🔹 5. CEOs earn hundreds of times more than workers.
    In 2023, the average CEO made over 300 times more than the average employee in the same company.
  • 🔹 6. Workers often have no vote or voice.
    Unlike cooperatives, most corporations don’t allow employees to vote on leadership or policies.
  • 🔹 7. Corporate influence often outweighs community needs.
    Decisions like outsourcing or factory closures prioritize profits over the well-being of workers or local economies.
  • 🔹 8. Companies use branding to distract from hierarchy.
    Brands like Apple or Starbucks promote progressive values, even while operating under highly centralized power structures.
  • 🔹 9. Gig workers have few protections or rights.
    Uber, DoorDash, and other gig platforms avoid providing benefits by classifying workers as independent contractors.
  • 🔹 10. Corporate media can shape public opinion.
    A small number of conglomerates own most media outlets, influencing what information is shared and how it's framed.
