Hierarchy in Corporations

  • 1. Corporations follow strict hierarchies—CEO at the top, middle managers, and employees at the base.
  • 2. Boards of Directors, often wealthy insiders, make high-level decisions most workers never see.
  • 3. Executives prioritize shareholders over workers or the environment—this is called "shareholder primacy."
  • 4. The top 1% in the U.S. hold over 30% of the nation's wealth, partly due to corporate structures.
  • 5. Corporations use their power to lobby for laws that benefit them—Amazon, Apple, and Meta spend millions annually.
  • 6. Worker voices are often silenced or ignored, especially in non-union environments.
  • 7. Corporate consolidation (mergers and acquisitions) reduces competition and increases centralized control.
  • 8. Executive pay has skyrocketed: CEOs make 300–400 times more than the average worker in many firms.
  • 9. Multinational corporations operate across borders, often bypassing national regulations and exploiting global labor.
