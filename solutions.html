💡 Solutions

How we can fix or reduce the problems caused by hierarchy.

What Can We Do?

  • 1. Reform campaign finance laws to reduce corporate and billionaire influence in politics.
  • 2. Enforce antitrust laws to break up monopolies and restore fair competition.
  • 3. Encourage worker cooperatives where employees have a vote in business decisions.
  • 4. Support unions that help balance power between employees and management.
  • 5. Increase government transparency with stronger whistleblower protections and open data laws.
  • 6. Push for ranked choice voting and election reform to give more people a voice.
  • 7. Improve civic education so people understand how power systems work and how to change them.
  • 8. Use technology to decentralize power (e.g., blockchain, open-source platforms).
  • 9. Advocate for corporate responsibility and ethical business models over shareholder-only profits.
  • 10. Build social movements that demand change and hold leaders accountable.

📚 Sources

  • 1. Hierarchies go back thousands of years—to monarchies, empires, and religious institutions.
  • 2. Many people are taught to respect authority and follow leaders without question.
  • 3. Hierarchical systems appear “natural” to many due to how society is structured (school, jobs, law).
  • 4. Governments are slow to change due to legal limits, gridlock, and deep bureaucracy.
  • 5. Corporations are protected under U.S. law as “legal persons,” giving them rights like free speech.
  • 6. The rich and powerful can afford lawyers, lobbyists, and media influence to keep control.
  • 7. Breaking up large systems is complex, risky, and expensive—so change is avoided.
  • 8. Technology and automation sometimes strengthen hierarchy by concentrating control in fewer hands.
  • 9. Globalization has made corporations even more powerful, able to shift jobs and avoid regulation.
  • 10. People may feel powerless or too busy to organize against large-scale hierarchy.
