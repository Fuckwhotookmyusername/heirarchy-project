Hierarchy in Government

  • 1. Most governments are structured with top-down authority—executives, legislatures, and courts at the top.
  • 2. This setup mirrors monarchies and colonial rule, where a few controlled the many.
  • 3. Even in democracies, political elites dominate elections due to money, media, and connections.
  • 4. Large bureaucracies enforce policies, but they often move slowly and are hard to reform.
  • 5. Lobbyists and special interest groups influence legislation, often more than everyday voters.
  • 6. The Electoral College and gerrymandering in the U.S. reduce direct representation.
  • 7. Citizens technically have voting power, but systemic barriers like voter suppression still exist.
  • 8. Laws and constitutions rarely allow average people to directly participate in lawmaking.
  • 9. Politicians often stay in power for decades, reinforcing hierarchical continuity.
